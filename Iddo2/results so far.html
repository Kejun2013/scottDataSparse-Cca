OK, no correlation with transcriptomic data yet, but I did do some taxonomic analysis on th metagenome. The raw data is on MG-RAST. If you open an account there, I can share the raw data with you online. http://metagenomics.nmpdr.org/

Basically I took each metagenomic sample and looked for alignments with Greengenes, which is a SSU rRNA database. I then took the top taxa found in all the samples (breastfed and formula-fed). Those were firmicutes, actinobacteria, proteobacteria and bacteroidetes. In two samples there were also a large number of verrucomicrobia: which might be close enough to proteobacteria for us to pool them together, not sure. Will check.

Parameters used:
sequence identity cutoff to Greengenes: 96%
alignment length >= 100 bp

In any case, I pooled all the samples of each treatment together, and looked to see whether there are significant differences between the two populations, using chi-squared test. 

Here is the table of the pooled samples for the four top taxa:

                                   FF                  BF
Firmicutes                   1197                305
Actinobacteria                838               1361
Proteobacteria                319                659
Bacteroidetes                   3                  549

The chi-square for goodness of fit rejected the hypothesis that the samples came from the same population. As you can see, the dominant species in the breast-fed (BF) column are actinobacteria, as opposed to Firmicutes in formula-fed. Also there are hardly any bacteroidetes in the formula-fed infants.

I am attaching two  spreadsheets, one for each sample. THey should be pretty self-explanatory, but do call me. One concern si that a chi-square test for homogeneity within each groups fails: that is, formula-fed babies are not homogeneous in their gut taxa, neither are breastfed. I am not sure that this is a problem though: population variation exists, and we can still "legally" pool the samples. However, I do appreciate a statistician looking at this, and discussing it with me. Let me know if you have any concerns about the way I approached this.
